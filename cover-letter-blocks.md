# Cover letter building blocks

Reusable, fact-checked components. Each cover letter should be custom: pick the blocks that fit the role, swap in the company/team, cut the rest. Every claim here is sourced from the impact-evidence dig, so none of it will fold under questioning. Do not invent numbers.

**Rules when assembling:** no em-dashes, no "passionate/leverage/robust" filler, lead with the point, vary sentence length. One page max. Name the specific team or product you're applying to in the opening and closing.

---

## Openers (pick one, then customize)

**A. Platform/tooling roles**
> I build the tooling other engineers build on. At Carta I created Verdict, an LLM-evaluation framework now used by four teams, and authored the domain-registry pattern every one of our MCP tools follows. I'm applying for [ROLE] because [SPECIFIC THING ABOUT THEIR PLATFORM/PRODUCT].

**B. Roles that value range**
> Ten years in, I've shipped across the stack: frontend architecture and design systems, a regulated filings pipeline spanning two countries, and lately the AI developer tooling my org runs on. [COMPANY]'s [SPECIFIC PROBLEM] is the kind of broad, ambiguous problem I like.

**C. Frontend-leaning roles**
> My craft is frontend and developer experience, and I've spent the last year proving it scales: I led a codebase-wide migration to react-query and built the AI tooling that keeps our agents from regressing. [ROLE] at [COMPANY] lines up with both.

---

## The Staff-level proof (use when the role is Staff or Staff-adjacent)

This is your honest answer to "why Staff when your title says Senior." It's peer-validated, not self-asserted.

> My title is Senior, but my last review landed at Exceeding Expectations with 5/5 from all three peer reviewers, and the written feedback describes me as already operating at the staff engineer level. I'm looking for a role that matches where the work already is.

Keep it to two sentences. Don't oversell it; the restraint is what makes it land.

---

## AI-platform ownership (the strongest block)

> I created Verdict, a framework for regression-testing AI agent behavior. Four teams adopted it, 74 plugin test suites are written against it, and in one refactor sweep it caught four regressions before they merged. Our CEO reviewed it personally. Separately, I authored the domain-registry architecture that every Carta MCP tool now follows; the MCP tech lead credited the pattern publicly, and I built the tool-search gateway and the architecture decision records the platform runs on.

Trim to whichever half fits. Verdict alone is a complete story; so is the MCP pattern.

---

## Teaching and adoption (use for roles that mention mentorship, culture, or DX)

> Building the tools was half the job. I ran recurring sessions teaching engineers across several teams, and other directors' orgs, how to work with AI coding agents, and I presented the platform to senior leadership including our CEO and a VP of Engineering. I care about whether people can actually use what I build.

---

## Frontend depth (foundation, not headline)

> Before the AI work, I spent years on frontend architecture: design systems, Storybook and visual-regression setups, and an AngularJS-to-React migration I led with a strangler approach. Most recently I ran a 3.5-month migration off a legacy data-fetching hook to react-query, now used in 224 files and maintained by nine other engineers. The frontend instinct is what makes the tooling usable.

---

## Product delivery (use when the role is product-facing, not pure infra)

> I don't only build internal tooling. I delivered Carta's International Securities filings end to end, HMRC in the UK and ATO in Australia, including an LLM autofill pipeline for government document templates. Regulated, externally visible, shipped.

---

## Customization checklist (before you send)

- [ ] Named the actual company and role in opener and closer
- [ ] Referenced something specific about their product/team (not generic praise)
- [ ] Cut every block that doesn't serve this role
- [ ] Chose Staff proof block only if the role is Staff-level
- [ ] One page, no em-dashes, no filler words
- [ ] Read it aloud once
